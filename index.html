Growth Strategy & Forecasting Framework
Situation
Joined Exploding Kittens with stagnant social + paid ads. No CTV, no OOH, little data infrastructure. Had to build process from scratch.
Task
In first 90 days ‚Üí run rapid tests, tie every initiative to measurable results. Set baselines: daily sales as control. Define success metrics: CPV, CAC, margin thresholds.
Action
Built framework for scaling. Example: Influencer Campaign (bottom funnel) - If efficiency metrics met (ex: $0.02 CPV) ‚Üí scale. If not ‚Üí pivot. Expanded into new top-of-funnel channels (Pinterest, CTV - leaning into awareness). Aligned campaigns with retail moments (seasonality, holidays).
Result
600M organic views. CTV ads with 10% lift. Made channels more profitable. Built trusted, repeatable system tied to business goals.
Follow-Up Prep
Validation: Set baselines first (daily sales control), defined clear metrics before spending. Leading indicator: CPV hitting target ($0.02) told me to scale vs pivot. What I'd change: Would have built the framework even faster - spent too long on influencer before pivoting to CTV.

Cohort Analysis - Email vs Viral
Situation
At Exploding Kittens, wanted to understand how different cohorts behaved. Compared email cohort (CRM buyers) vs viral cohort (buyers after TikTok spikes).
Action
Email cohort = customers who already converted (checkout or free gift sign-up). Goal = expand LTV through launches + cross-sells. Viral cohort = new buyers from awareness/FOMO spikes. Goal = acquisition, more novelty-driven.
Result
Email cohort = lower incremental cost to activate (we already owned the channel), more consistent repeat behavior. Viral cohort = higher first AOV but weaker repeat rates.
Takeaway
Treat viral cohorts as acquisition drivers, email cohorts as retention + LTV expansion drivers. At Relatable, email wasn't really used this way - missed opportunity that reshaped my CRM approach.
Follow-Up Prep
Validation: Tracked both cohorts separately from day one with different goals/metrics. Leading indicator: Email repeat purchase rate vs viral one-time buyer rate showed clear divergence early. What I'd change: Would have recognized this pattern earlier at Relatable - could have built email as retention driver sooner.

CTV Easter Test - Channel Diversification
Situation
At EK, tried influencer campaign ‚Üí didn't hit pre-set metrics like CPV or lift targets. Pivoted to test CTV for kids' game "Hurry Up, Chicken Butt."
Action
Ran test during Easter period. Tailored campaign to families. Ran 2-week CTV spot. Used UK as geo holdout for comparison (treated as control).
Result
US sales = 10% lift YoY vs UK holdout. Clear proof CTV drove incremental seasonal sales. Scaled with 2x-3x budget increase, close monitoring. Now pushing across YouTube as well.
Follow-Up Prep
Validation: Used UK as geo holdout (control) - clean test design before committing. Leading indicator: 10% lift YoY in US vs UK showed it was working within 2 weeks. What I'd change: Would have tested CTV before influencer campaign - learned influencer didn't hit metrics first, could have saved time/budget.
Survivor CTV Partnership - Upper Funnel
Situation
Launched official Survivor game, needed to capture superfans and casual family viewers of reality TV. Wanted upper-funnel activation tied to show's audience.
Action
Partnered with CBS to have Jeff Probst promote game in commercial interstitials during season. Frequency naturally limited to one exposure every couple weeks (accepted going in). Set sales baseline and tracked results on Amazon and retail.
Result
Every time interstitial ran ‚Üí clear, immediate sales lift. Amazon captured majority of spike. Since it was only marketing effort outside season, attribution was very clean. Proved limited, upper-funnel exposure drove measurable incremental sales.
Reflection
Learned frequency management looks different depending on context. Not about multiple touchpoints - about single, high-impact placement.
Follow-Up Prep
Validation: Set sales baseline before partnership, accepted limited frequency upfront as part of test design. Leading indicator: Immediate Amazon sales spike every time interstitial aired - clear, repeatable pattern. What I'd change: Would negotiate for more frequency in contract if possible, but learned one high-quality placement can be more effective than many low-quality ones.

Sizzle Reel Production - Clear Ownership
Situation
At Exploding Kittens, international distributors often request sizzle reels in batches of 6-8 to pitch games to their markets. Projects tended to "sneak up" because we didn't know their pitch timelines. Before I joined, deadlines were often missed and reels weren't strong sales tools.
Action
Mapped production plan to ensure no bottlenecks: Copywriter ‚Üí scripting, Social strategist ‚Üí filming, Contractor ‚Üí editing, Me ‚Üí approvals, Marketing coordinator ‚Üí deployment. Set clear DRIs (Directly Responsible Individuals) for each step, built in check-ins, flagged risks like evolving game art/mechanics. Ran post-mortems after each batch to refine timelines and processes.
Result
Went from missing deadlines to consistently hitting them, even under tight timelines. Reels improved dramatically - using experience from PDPs and CTV, shifted creative toward FOMO-driven, clear explanations that made buyers want to pick up games, even with lower production budgets.
Follow-Up Prep
Validation: Mapped dependencies and set clear DRIs before starting production. Leading indicator: Check-in milestones hitting on time showed system working. What I'd change: Would have implemented post-mortems from batch one - took a few rounds to optimize the process fully.
