Cohort Analysis - Email vs Viral
Situation
At Exploding Kittens, wanted to understand how different cohorts behaved. Compared email cohort (CRM buyers) vs viral cohort (buyers after TikTok spikes).
Action
Email cohort = customers who already converted (checkout or free gift sign-up). Goal = expand LTV through launches + cross-sells. Viral cohort = new buyers from awareness/FOMO spikes. Goal = acquisition, more novelty-driven.
Result
Email cohort = lower incremental cost to activate (we already owned the channel), more consistent repeat behavior. Viral cohort = higher first AOV but weaker repeat rates.
Takeaway
Treat viral cohorts as acquisition drivers, email cohorts as retention + LTV expansion drivers. At Relatable, email wasn't really used this way - missed opportunity that reshaped my CRM approach.

Sizzle Reel Production - Clear Ownership
Situation
At Exploding Kittens, international distributors often request sizzle reels in batches of 6-8 to pitch games to their markets. Projects tended to "sneak up" because we didn't know their pitch timelines. Before I joined, deadlines were often missed and reels weren't strong sales tools.
Action
Mapped production plan to ensure no bottlenecks: Copywriter ‚Üí scripting, Social strategist ‚Üí filming, Contractor ‚Üí editing, Me ‚Üí approvals, Marketing coordinator ‚Üí deployment. Set clear DRIs (Directly Responsible Individuals) for each step, built in check-ins, flagged risks like evolving game art/mechanics. Ran post-mortems after each batch to refine timelines and processes.
Result
Went from missing deadlines to consistently hitting them, even under tight timelines. Reels improved dramatically - using experience from PDPs and CTV, shifted creative toward FOMO-driven, clear explanations that made buyers want to pick up games, even with lower production budgets.
